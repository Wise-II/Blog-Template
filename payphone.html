Last payphone in NYC was removed yesterday

Published 24 hours ago tag category 3 comments

New York City's last payphone was decommissioned yesterday. It happened near the intersection of 7th Avenue and 50th Street in Manhattan's Midtown. A crane may be seen loading it onto a flatbed vehicle. A minor ceremony was conducted near Times Square, down the street. It is being referred to as the end of an era. According to the most recent statistics, there were roughly 100,000 payphones in the United States in 2018. This compares to two million in 1999. And around 20% of them were in New York. New York started removing them in 2015, because cell phones have made them obsolete.
