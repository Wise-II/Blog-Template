Lawsuit filed against DeSantis, Florida over bill critics call ‘Don’t Say Gay’

Published 3 months ago tag category 3 comments

Civil rights groups, including LGBTQ advocacy group Equality Florida, joined the National Center for Lesbian Rights and families in Florida in filing suit against the state of Florida on Thursday. Among the individual plaintiffs are a Miami rabbi and her partner. The 80-page lawsuit alleges that the new law is an unconstitutional effort to destroy LGBTQ students and families from schools. Equality Florida issued a statement calling DeSantis’ law “cruel” and saying it is “meant to isolate, stigmatize, and erase LGBTQ families.” The challenge filed in federal court in Tallahassee contends that the law violates the First Amendment and other provisions of the U.S. Constitution. The bill is HB1557, the Parental Rights in Education, among other stipulations prohibits classroom discussion about sexual orientation or gender identity in Kindergarden Through 3rd grade.
